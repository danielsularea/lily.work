The Nanny
Slow and plump
she moves through the kitchen,
cooking dishes I don’t like
with a hum in her throat.
Spinach, ginger, egg yolks
and chicken,
which is in the bathroom,
still clucking.
The pot boils and
the nanny hurries
she grabs the beast by its neck—
coos at it while it struggles.
Stretched nicely across the counter
she brings down the knife
and the chicken shivers.


She sees me—I’ve been watching,
and those eyes, again
look for her children.
You’re picky, she says,
and ungrateful.
Won’t eat this or that,
my brats eat everything
she laughs,
they’re grateful.


The chicken bleeds
and she washes it.
With her uneven nails,
she pinches its feathers
and plucks them out.
​When it is bare
she puts it into the pot
and gives me the leftover head
Play with that, the nanny says,
and pushes me out of her kitchen,
with the door closed behind me.
Again, she hums.
